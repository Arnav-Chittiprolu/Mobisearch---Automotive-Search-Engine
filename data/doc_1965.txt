The '''Polestar 2''' is a [[Battery electric vehicle|battery electric]] 5-door [[liftback]]<!-- A "fastback" is a broad styling term used to describe any car with an uninterrupted slope in the roofline from the roof to the rear bumper, not describing the specific body style --> marketed by [[Polestar]], an affiliate of [[Geely Holding]] and [[Volvo Cars]]. Based on the [[Compact Modular Architecture platform|CMA platform]], production began in March 2020<ref>{{Cite press release |url=https://www.polestar.com/us/press/press-release/production-of-the-polestar-2-electric-vehicle-begins |title=Production of the Polestar 2 Electric Vehicle Begins |publisher=Polestar |access-date=10 April 2020 |archive-date=10 April 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200410174144/https://www.polestar.com/us/press/press-release/production-of-the-polestar-2-electric-vehicle-begins |url-status=live }}</ref> at the [[List of Volvo Car production plants|Luqiao CMA Super Factory]] in [[Luqiao]], Zhejiang<!-- DO NOT LINK, see [[MOS:GEOLINK]] for further guidance -->, China.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://insideevs.com/news/345748/polestar-2-debuts-in-china-will-be-produced-in-luqiao-from-2020/ |title=Polestar 2 Debuts in China: Will Be Produced In Luqiao From 2020 |website=[[Motorsport Network#InsideEVs|InsideEVs]] |first=Mark |last=Kane |date=14 April 2019 |access-date=26 April 2020 |archive-date=8 May 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190508093505/https://insideevs.com/news/345748/polestar-2-debuts-in-china-will-be-produced-in-luqiao-from-2020/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

Polestar 2 is the second car under the Polestar brand after the [[Polestar 1]]. Polestar 1 is, however, a [[plug-in hybrid]] and limited production vehicle, making the Polestar 2 the first [[battery electric vehicle]] and mass production Polestar vehicle.{{citation needed|date=October 2019}}

===2024 facelift===
[[File:Polestar 2 (Facelift) IMG 9469.jpg|thumb|left|Polestar 2 2024 facelift]]

An updated Polestar 2 was revealed in January 2023 for the 2024 model year, with rear-wheel drive becoming the standard drivetrain for the entry-level model instead of the previous front-wheel drive.  The single motor model was upgraded to produce {{cvt|299|hp|PS kW}} and {{Convert|361|lbft|Nm|0|abbr=on}} of torque, an increase of {{cvt|68|hp|PS kW}} and {{Convert|118|lbft|Nm|0|abbr=on}}, resulting in a 1.1 second faster {{Cvt|0-60|mph|km/h}} time â€“ now 5.9 seconds. The long range trim's battery was also upgraded to a capacity of 82 kWh. The powertrain of the dual-motor model's output was raised by {{cvt|13|hp|PS kW}} and {{Convert|79|lbft|Nm|0|abbr=on}} for a total of {{cvt|421|hp|PS kW}} and {{Convert|546|lbft|Nm|0|abbr=on}}, enabling a {{Cvt|0-60|mph|km/h}} time of 4.3 seconds. Dual-motor models, now using an [[Induction motor|asynchronous motor]] in the front, will be able to disengage the front axle to increase efficiency and range.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |last=Day |first=Lewin |date=24 January 2023 |title=2024 Polestar 2 Switches from FWD to RWD in a Surprise Move |url=https://www.thedrive.com/news/2024-polestar-2-gets-more-power-more-range-and-rear-wheel-drive |access-date=3 August 2023 |website=The Drive |language=en |archive-date=11 June 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230611013629/https://www.thedrive.com/news/2024-polestar-2-gets-more-power-more-range-and-rear-wheel-drive |url-status=live }}</ref>

The front end also received an update, exchanging its grid-like grille for a more covered version, with an array of sensors and a camera in the center. In addition, the Pilot Pack will come standard for dual-motor models in the North American market.<ref name=":0" />

== Recalls ==
In October 2020, Polestar issued a recall on all Polestar 2 models due to a software defect that caused loss of power to the vehicle whilst driving. As the vehicles were not capable of [[Over-the-air programming|OTA]] software updates at that time it required all of the vehicles to be recalled to a service centre for a manual software update.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.caranddriver.com/news/a34276283/polestar-2-ev-recall/|title=All Polestar 2s Recalled for Software Glitch That Can Shut Down the EV|first=Roberto|last=Baldwin|date=29 October 2020|website=Car and Driver|access-date=13 October 2020|archive-date=13 October 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201013172856/https://www.caranddriver.com/news/a34276283/polestar-2-ev-recall/|url-status=live}}</ref>

Later the same month a second recall was issued due to faulty inverters.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://robbreport.com/motors/cars/polestar-2-second-recall-glitch-inverter-1234578366/|title=The New Polestar 2 EV Was Just Recalled for the Second Time in a Month|first1=Bryan|last1=Hood|date=30 October 2020|website=robbreport.com|access-date=1 November 2020|archive-date=2 November 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201102002442/https://robbreport.com/motors/cars/polestar-2-second-recall-glitch-inverter-1234578366/|url-status=live}}</ref>

From September 2021 to March 2022, the Polestar 2 Single Motor Standard Range with a 64&nbsp;kWh battery and 165&nbsp;kW power, 224&nbsp;hp was available in Europe. The battery for this version was made by [[CATL]]. The 64&nbsp;kWh and 165&nbsp;kW power version is available until now in China, as the only Single Motor Standard Range version.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://ev-database.uk/car/1486/Polestar-2-Standard-Range-Single-Motor |title=Polestar 2 Standard Range Single Motor |website=EV Database |access-date=22 December 2022 |archive-date=2 December 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221202183334/https://ev-database.uk/car/1486/Polestar-2-Standard-Range-Single-Motor |url-status=live }}</ref>

Starting with the 2025 Model Year, only the Dual-motor variants will be available in North America.<ref>{{Cite web |title=The Polestar 2 Just Got More Expensive For 2025 |url=https://insideevs.com/news/740590/polestar-2-2025-price-range/#:~:text=The%20only%20Polestar%202%20available,version%20with%20the%20Performance%20Pack |access-date=2024-11-19 |website=InsideEVs |language=en}}</ref>

=== Environment ===
Using the [[Life-cycle assessment#Main ISO phases of LCA|ISO 14044]] method, Polestar 2 has lower [[Life-cycle greenhouse gas emissions of energy sources|life-cycle emissions]] than the XC40 when driving more than {{convert|35000|to|80000|km|mi}}, depending on conditions.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Valle |first1=Marius |title=Mener Polestars egen analyse stiller bensinbilen i et bedre lys enn den fortjener |url=https://www.tu.no/artikler/mener-polestars-egen-analyse-stiller-bensinbilen-i-et-bedre-lys-enn-den-fortjener/499472 |website=Tu.no |publisher=[[Teknisk Ukeblad]] |language=no |date=17 September 2020 |access-date=18 September 2020 |archive-date=18 September 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200918163159/https://www.tu.no/artikler/mener-polestars-egen-analyse-stiller-bensinbilen-i-et-bedre-lys-enn-den-fortjener/499472 |url-status=live }}</ref>

== Delivery ==
The Polestar 2 is produced in a factory located in [[Chengdu]], China.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Polestar |first1=Cars |title=Production |url=https://about.polestar.com/production/ |publisher=Polestar |language=en |date=31 October 2022 |access-date=31 October 2022 |archive-date=31 October 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221031190626/https://about.polestar.com/production/ |url-status=live }}</ref> All cars for the European market get shipped to Shanghai, China, by train. In Shanghai, the car gets placed on a boat to [[Zeebrugge]], Belgium. In Zeebrugge, the European cars get approved after European new-car rules before getting shipped to the destination country.<ref>{{cite web |title=Car delivery questions |url=https://www.polestar.com/polestar-support/ |publisher=Polestar |language=en |date=31 October 2022 |access-date=31 October 2022 |archive-date=20 July 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230720220838/https://www.polestar.com/en-ca/polestar-support/ |url-status=live }}</ref>
