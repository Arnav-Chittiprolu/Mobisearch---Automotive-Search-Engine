In 1991, Volkswagen launched the third-generation Golf, which was [[European Car of the Year]] for 1992. The [[Volkswagen Golf Mk3|Golf Mk3]] and [[Volkswagen Vento (A3)|Jetta Mk3]] arrived in North America in 1993. The sedan version of the Golf was badged Vento in Europe but remained Jetta in the United States. The [[Volkswagen Scirocco|Scirocco]] and the later [[Volkswagen Corrado|Corrado]] were both Golf-based [[coupÃ©]]s.

[[File:2002 Volkswagen New Beetle (9C MY02.5) 2.0 coupe (2010-10-01) 01.jpg|thumb|right|The [[Volkswagen New Beetle]]]]

In 1994, Volkswagen unveiled the [[J Mays]]-designed [[Volkswagen New Beetle#History|Concept One]], a "retro"-themed [[concept car]] with a resemblance to the original Beetle, based on the platform of the Polo. Due to a positive response to the concept, a production version was developed as the [[Volkswagen New Beetle|New Beetle]], based on the Golf's larger platform.<ref name="ltvvwc-2003">{{cite web|title=The VW Experimental Vehicles â€“ the 1980s to present pt 2|url=http://www.ltv-vwc.org.uk/wheelspin/WS_feb_2003/WnW-prototypes04.htm|work=Wheelspin|publisher=London & Thames Valley VW Club|access-date=12 July 2010|date=February 2003|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110614094520/http://www.ltv-vwc.org.uk/wheelspin/WS_feb_2003/WnW-prototypes04.htm|archive-date=14 June 2011}}</ref>

In 1995 the [[Volkswagen Sharan|Sharan]] was launched in Europe, the result of a joint venture with [[Ford Motor Company|Ford]], which also resulted in the [[Ford Galaxy]] and [[SEAT Alhambra]].<ref>{{cite web|title=VW Sharan|url=http://www.carmagazine.co.uk/Drives/Search-Results/First-drives/VW-Sharan-2011-CAR-review/|website=Car Magazine|access-date=4 January 2015|date=2 June 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150104171105/http://www.carmagazine.co.uk/Drives/Search-Results/First-drives/VW-Sharan-2011-CAR-review/|archive-date=4 January 2015}}</ref>

Other main models during the decade include the [[Volkswagen Polo|Polo]], a smaller car than the Golf, and the larger [[Volkswagen Passat|Passat]] for the segment above the Golf.
