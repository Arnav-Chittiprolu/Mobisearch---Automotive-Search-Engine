From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia Japanese entrepreneur An early photo of Risaburo before his death Risaburo Toyoda ( 豊田 利三郎 , Toyoda Risaburō [ 1 ] ; March 5, 1884 – June 3, 1952) was a Japanese entrepreneur. He was the son-in-law of Toyota Industries Co., Ltd founder Sakichi Toyoda , and brother-in-law of Toyota Motor Corporation founder, Kiichiro Toyoda ( 豊田 喜一郎 , Toyoda Kiichirō ; June 11, 1894 – March 27, 1952) His original surname was Kodama (児玉). He graduated from Kobe University (then Kobe Koto Shogyo Gakko ) and Hitotsubashi University (then Tokyo Koto Shogyo Gakko ) .In 1939, he became the first president of the Toyota Motor Corporation. [ 2 ] He died at the age of 68 in 1952.
