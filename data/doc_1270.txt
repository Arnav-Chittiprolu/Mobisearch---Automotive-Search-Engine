Do not fill this in! ====Dual-Motor====
In March 2022, Rivian announced it would develop and sell a dual-motor powertrain for the R1T, with one electric motor for each axle. The price of the dual-motor (DM) version would start around the same initial price at which the quad-motor (QM) version was offered, while the QM version would increase in price by almost 20%. The output of the DM version was expected to be more than {{cvt|600|hp}} and {{cvt|600|lbft}} of torque,<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.cnet.com/roadshow/news/rivian-dual-motor-r1s-r1t-launch-quad-motor-price-increase/ |title=Rivian Launches 600-HP Dual-Motor R1S and R1T, Raises Quad-Motor Pricing |author=Golson, Daniel |date=March 1, 2022 |work=Road/Show |publisher=CNet |access-date=May 26, 2023}}</ref> but was later published by Rivian at {{cvt|533|hp}} and {{cvt|610|lbft}} of torque.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://stories.rivian.com/difference-between-r1-drive-systems | title=Rivian Drive Systems, Decoded - Rivian Stories }}</ref> The largest ("Max") battery was removed as an option for the QM version in December, limiting availability to DM versions only.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://insideevs.com/news/627793/rivian-r1t-r1s-no-quad-motor-max-pack-2023/ |title=Rivian Removes Variant Of Its R1T For 2023, Provides Other Updates |author=Loveday, Steven |date=December 21, 2022 |work=Inside EVs |access-date=May 26, 2023}}</ref> The "Enduro" traction motors for the DM versions were developed by Rivian in-house and reduce the number of semiconductors used compared to the QM, alleviating a key component supply restraint;<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.carscoops.com/2023/04/rivian-is-making-its-own-new-motors-to-speed-up-production-and-reduce-delays/ |title=Rivian Is Making Its Own New Motors To Speed Up Production And Reduce Delays |author=Bell, Sebastien |date=April 14, 2023 |work=CarScoops |access-date=May 30, 2023}}</ref> production began in February 2023,<ref name=IEVs-20230224>{{cite news |url=https://insideevs.com/news/654215/rivian-starts-production-enduro-motors-for-dual-motor-awd-models/ |title=Rivian Starts Production Of Enduro Motors For Dual-Motor AWD Models |author=Mihalascu, Dan |date=February 24, 2023 |work=Inside EVs |access-date=May 26, 2023}}</ref> although most of the initial production were diverted to [[Rivian EDV]]s.

In April 2023, Rivian introduced a "Performance Dual-Motor" option (PDM), which uses software to unlock enhanced performance from the DM motors. With the PDM option, the R1T will produce approximately {{cvt|700|hp}} and {{cvt|700|lbft}} of torque.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theverge.com/2023/4/7/23673961/rivian-performance-dual-motor-availability-max-pack-battery |title=Rivian's new 'Performance' powertrain lets R1T reservation holders keep their big battery |author=Shakir, Umar |date=April 7, 2023 |work=The Verge |access-date=May 26, 2023}}</ref> As tested by ''Motor Trend'', each PDM [[traction motor]] has a maximum output of {{cvt|350|hp}} and {{cvt|428|lbft}}; combined output is slightly less than double, at {{cvt|665|hp}} and {{cvt|829|lbft}}. Externally, the PDM (silver accented badging, black brake calipers) can be distinguished from the QM (yellow accented badging and yellow brake calipers).<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.motortrend.com/reviews/2023-rivian-r1t-dual-motor-performance-first-test-review/ |title=2023 Rivian R1T Dual-Motor Performance First Test: It's the Same Picture |author=Evans, Scott |author2=Walker, William (photographer) |date=July 13, 2023 |work=Motor Trend |access-date=July 14, 2023}}</ref>

The first R1T DM was completed on May 25, 2023, with deliveries to commence in June.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://stories.rivian.com/dual-motor-r1t-start-of-production |title=First Customer Dual-Motor R1T rolls off the line |date=May 25, 2023 |publisher=Rivian Automotive |access-date=May 26, 2023}}</ref>
