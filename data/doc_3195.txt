Mufflers are installed within the [[exhaust system]] of most [[internal combustion engine]]s.<ref name="how">{{cite web| first=Karim |last=Nice |title=How Mufflers Work |date=19 February 2001 |website=auto.howstuffworks.com |url= https://auto.howstuffworks.com/muffler.htm/printable |access-date=3 February 2023}}</ref> Mufflers are engineered as an acoustic device to reduce the [[loudness]] of the [[sound pressure]] created by the engine by [[acoustic quieting]]. Sound reduction techniques used in mufflers include: reactive silencing, resistive silencing, absorptive silencing, and shell damping.<ref>{{cite web |last1=JÃ¤Ã¤skelÃ¤inen |first1=Hannu |title=Diesel Exhaust Systems |url= https://dieselnet.com/tech/diesel_exh_sys.php |website=dieselnet.com |date=2008 |access-date=4 February 2023}}</ref> The noise of the hot exhaust gas exiting the engine can be abated by a series of passages and chambers lined with roving [[fiberglass]] insulation and/or resonating chambers [[harmonic]]ally tuned to cause [[destructive interference]], wherein opposite sound waves cancel each other out.<ref>{{cite web |title=Interference of Waves |url= http://physics.bu.edu/~duffy/py105/WaveInterference.html |website=physics.bu.edu |date=3 March 1999 |access-date=3 February 2023}}</ref>

The operation of an internal combustion engine produces distinct pulses of exhaust gas that exit through the exhaust pipes and the muffler.<ref name="DoWeNeed">{{cite web |title=Exhaust Backpressure: Do We Need It? |url= https://trade.mechanic.com.au/news/exhaust-backpressure-do-we-need-it1 |website=trade.mechanic.com.au |date=23 May 2019 |access-date=3 February 2023}}</ref> For example, a four-cylinder engine will have four high-pressure pulses for each operating cycle, a six-cylinder engine will emit six high-pressure pulses, and so on.<ref name="DoWeNeed"/> These pulses are separated by a low-pressure pulse between them which functions as a scavenging mechanism for the next exhaust cycle from the cylinder.<ref name="DoWeNeed"/> The exhaust system needs negative pressure waves so they help empty the cylinder of gases.<ref name="Glucker"/> The more distinct separate pulses in the exhaust system, the more positive the exhaust flow, and the more efficiently the exhaust gas is scavenged from each cylinder.<ref name="DoWeNeed"/>

The design of the exhaust systems and mufflers must compromise effective exhaust gas extraction and exhaust gas pressure, engine [[fuel efficiency]] and power, as well as noise suppression.{{cn|date=February 2023}} A side effect of noise reduction is the restriction of the exhaust gas flow, which creates [[back pressure]], which can decrease [[engine efficiency]]. This is because the engine exhaust must share the same complex exit pathway built inside the muffler as the sound pressure that the muffler is designed to mitigate. However, having some back pressure helps.<ref name="Glucker">{{cite web |last1=Glucker |first1=Jeff |title=Do vehicle exhaust systems need back pressure? |url= https://www.motorauthority.com/news/1114543_do-vehicle-exhaust-systems-need-back-pressure |work=Motor Authority |date=2 January 2018 |access-date=3 February 2023}}</ref> Higher backpressure can also help [[nitrous oxide]]s (NOx) emission reduction in some engines.<ref>{{cite journal| title=A review on the correlation between exhaust backpressure and the performance of IC engine |first=R. |last=Murali1 |display-authors=etal |journal=Journal of Physics: Conference Series |volume=2051 |date=23 August 2021 |article-number=012044 |location=Perlis, Malaysia |doi=10.1088/1742-6596/2051/1/012044 |s2cid=239878490 |doi-access=free }}</ref>

When engine performance is the main concern, the exhaust pipes and muffler should be large enough to facilitate breathing as well as small enough to create a high exhaust flow.<ref name="Glucker"/> The objective of a muffled high-performance exhaust system depends on two independent factors: "the pressure wave tuning from length/diameter selection, and minimizing backpressure by selecting mufflers of suitable flow capacity for the application."<ref name="Demystified">{{cite web |title=Auto Exhaust Science - Exhaust Science Demystified |url= https://www.motortrend.com/how-to/0505phr-exh/ |work=Motor Trend |date=16 May 2005 |access-date=4 February 2023}}</ref>

Some [[Aftermarket (automotive)|aftermarket]] mufflers claim to increase engine output and/or reduce [[Fuel economy in automobiles|fuel consumption]] by slightly reduced back pressure.{{CN|date=February 2023}} This usually entails less noise reduction (i.e., more noise).
