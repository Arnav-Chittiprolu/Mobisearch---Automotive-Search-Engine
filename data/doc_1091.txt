=== Audi R8 E-Tron (2015) ===
[[File:Audi R8 e-Tron.JPG|thumb|Audi R8 e-Tron]]
The R8 E-Tron was an electric version of the [[Audi R8]]. It got to 100 kilometers an hour in under 4 seconds, and a claimed range of over 280 miles.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.audi-mediacenter.com/en/audi-r8-e-tron-74|title=Audi R8 e-tron (2015)|website=Audi MediaCenter}}</ref> Less than 100 were produced, making it a limited production vehicle.

=== Q8 e-tron (2018) ===
{{Main|Audi Q8 e-tron}}
[[File:Audi Q8 e-tron IAA 2023 1X7A0616.jpg|thumb|[[Audi Q8 e-tron]] is based on the [[Volkswagen Group MLB platform|MLB]] platform.]]
An [[Sport utility vehicle|SUV]] formerly called the "Audi e-tron", with a 328-kilometer (204-mile) EPA range and a 95-kWh battery, [[Audi Brussels|entered production]] in 2018<ref>{{cite web|url= https://electrek.co/2016/12/26/10-electric-cars/ |title=10 electric cars coming in the next 3 years |first=Fred |last=Lambert |date= 2016-12-26 |work=Electrek |access-date= 2016-12-26 |quote=The vehicle is expected to follow the Q6 in 2019-2020)}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= https://electrek.co/2017/06/12/audi-electric-car-factory/ |title=Audi previews new electric car factory: 'Electric motors instead of internal combustion engines, batteries instead of fuel tanks' |first=Fred |last=Lambert |work=Electrek |date=12 June 2017|access-date=12 June 2017}}</ref> and was first delivered in 2019. The vehicle is sold in many countries, including the US, Canada and multiple countries in Europe. At the end of September 2019, there were more than 10,000 e-trons registered worldwide.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://insideevs.com/news/374641/audi-e-tron-sales-us-declining/|title=Audi e-tron Sales In U.S. Continue To Decline In September|website=InsideEVs}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.autohaus.de/marktdaten/neuzulassungen-1377988.html | title=Neuzulassungen | publisher=Autohaus | language=de | access-date=24 January 2021 | archive-date=11 December 2020 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201211133148/https://www.autohaus.de/marktdaten/neuzulassungen-1377988.html | url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://eu-evs.com/|title=eu-evs.com|website=eu-evs.com}}</ref>

Audi announced that the facelifted e-tron will be renamed, and will be sold as the [[Audi Q8 e-tron]]; it was revealed on November 9, 2022. <ref>{{cite web |title=Audi confirms e-tron is dead, long live the fresh Q8 e-tron and Q8 Sportback e-tron |date=7 November 2022 |url=https://www.autoevolution.com/news/audi-confirms-e-tron-is-dead-long-live-the-fresh-q8-e-tron-and-q8-sportback-e-tron-203120.html |publisher=Autoevolution ([[Softpedia]])}}</ref>

=== Q2L e-tron (2019) ===
[[File:AUDI Q2L E-TRON China (2).jpg|thumb|Audi Q2L e-tron is based on the gasoline-powered vehicle [[Audi Q2]], which is based the [[Volkswagen Group MQB platform|MQB]] platform.]]
The Q2L e-tron is an all-electric version of the long-wheelbase variant of the [[subcompact SUV]] [[Audi Q2]], which went into production at the [[FAW-Volkswagen|Foshan]] plant in November 2019 and is sold exclusively in China. Powered by a 38 kWh lithium-ion battery of Chinese supplier [[Contemporary Amperex Technology]], the Q2L e-tron has a range of {{convert|265|km|mi|0}} on one charge and a top speed of {{convert|150|km/h|0|abbr=on}}. Its electric motor delivers the maximum power of {{convert|100|kW|0|abbr=on}} and maximum torque of {{convert|290|Nm|0|abbr=on}}.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.autonews.com/china/audi-launches-first-locally-built-ev|title=Audi launches first locally built EV|date=2019-11-25|website=Automotive News|language=en|access-date=2019-12-14}}</ref>

=== Q8 e-tron Sportback (2020) ===
{{Main|Audi Q8 e-tron#Sportback version}}
[[File:Audi Q8 e-tron Sportback 1X7A7094.jpg|alt=|thumb|[[Audi Q8 e-tron#Sportback version|Audi Q8 e-tron Sportback]] is based on the MLB platform.]]
The e-tron Sportback is an all-electric [[coupÃ©]] SUV shown first as a concept at the 2017 Shanghai Motor Show. It has the same powertrain/battery as the [[Audi e-tron (2018)|e-tron SUV]]. Both cars are produced in Brussels, Belgium.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://evcompare.io/cars/audi/audi_e-tron_sportback_concept/|title=Audi e-tron Sportback concept specs, photos, price, offers and incentives|website=EV Compare.io|language=en|access-date=2019-04-16|archive-date=16 April 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190416175103/https://evcompare.io/cars/audi/audi_e-tron_sportback_concept/|url-status=dead}}</ref> As in the e-tron SUV, the battery of the vehicle is liquid-cooled.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.audiusa.com/technology/efficiency/e-tron#/modal/modal-sportback-concept|title=Audi e-tronÂ® Electric Cars {{!}} Audi USA {{!}} Audi USA|website=www.audiusa.com|language=en|access-date=2019-04-16}}</ref>

The production version was revealed at the 2019 [[LA Auto Show|Los Angeles Auto Show]]. European and American deliveries started in 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://insideevs.com/news/447169/audi-etron-sales-us-record-q2-2020/|title=Audi e-tron Sales In U.S. Hit New Record In Q3 2020|website=InsideEVs}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.caranddriver.com/news/a29851281/audi-e-tron-sportback-2020-photos-info/|title=2020 Audi e-tron Sportback is the A7 of Audi EVs|work=[[Car and Driver]]|last=Frank|first=Michael|date=19 November 2019|access-date=19 November 2019}}</ref>

The 2020 e-tron Sportback has an EPA range of {{convert|218|mi|km|0|disp=flip}}.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fueleconomy.gov/feg/Find.do?action=sbs&id=42674|title=2020 Audi e-tron Sportback|website=www.fueleconomy.gov}}</ref> It is better than in the 2019 e-tron SUV, partially because a larger percentage of the battery capacity is usable. The e-tron's battery management system keeps part of the battery capacity as a buffer, which is not part of the usable capacity; now the size of that buffer was decreased to unlock more usable capacity. This change was introduced in the e-tron SUV during the production run, while in the e-tron Sportback, which entered production later, it was available from the beginning.

In 2022, it was announced that the vehicle will be renamed. It is now known as the Audi ''Q8'' e-tron Sportback.

=== e-tron GT (2021) ===
{{main|Audi e-tron GT}}
[[File:Audi RS e-tron GT.jpg|alt=Audi e-tron GT|thumb|Audi RS e-tron GT]]
The e-tron GT is a four-door all-electric [[grand tourer]]. The concept model was showcased at the [[Los Angeles Auto Show#2018|2018 Los Angeles Auto Show]]. It is built on the same platform as the [[Porsche Mission E|Porsche Taycan]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://jalopnik.com/the-2020-audi-e-tron-gt-heres-more-of-audis-electric-t-1830677784|title=The 2020 Audi E-Tron GT: Here's More of Audi's Electric Tesla-Fighter|first=Justin T.|last=Westbrook|website=Jalopnik |date=27 November 2018 }}</ref> It is fitted with an 800-volt system, which substantially reduces charging times. The vehicle is driven by two permanently excited synchronous [[electric motor]]s with a combined power output of {{convert|434|kW|hp|0|abbr=on}}. A 90-kWh lithium-ion battery powers the e-tron GT's electric engines and enables it to travel up to {{cvt|400|km|0|}} according to the new [[Worldwide harmonized light vehicles test procedure|WLTP]] standard.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://motorillustrated.com/la-auto-show-2018-audi-e-tron-gt-concept-is-just-plain-crazy/14598/|title=LA Auto Show 2018: Audi e-tron GT Concept is Just Plain Crazy|first=Charles|last=Jolicoeur|date=28 November 2018}}</ref>

The e-tron GT concept made an appearance in the 2019 film ''[[Avengers: Endgame]]'', in which [[Tony Stark (Marvel Cinematic Universe)|Tony Stark]] drives the e-tron GT in one scene in the film.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.autoblog.com/2018/12/04/audi-e-tron-gt-concept-avengers-4/|title=Audi E-Tron GT Concept confirmed for 'Avengers 4'|last=Markovich|first=Tony|publisher=[[Weblogs, Inc.|Autoblog.com]]|date=4 December 2018|access-date=11 December 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181205140403/https://www.autoblog.com/2018/12/04/audi-e-tron-gt-concept-avengers-4/|archive-date=5 December 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> Production model is announced for 9 February 2021.

=== Q4 e-tron (2021) ===
{{main|Audi Q4 e-tron}}
[[File:2021 Audi Q4 e-tron Sport 35.jpg|alt=Audi Q4 e-tron photo|thumb|Audi Q4 e-tron is based on the [[Volkswagen Group MEB platform|MEB]] platform.]]

The Q4 e-tron is a battery electric [[Crossover (automobile)|crossover SUV]] unveiled at the [[Geneva Motor Show#2019|2019 Geneva Motor Show]] as a concept model. According to the Audi, the use of VW's [[Volkswagen Group MEB platform|MEB]] platform should improve its affordability. The production model is released in April 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Langness|first=Travis|date=2021-01-04|title=2021 Audi e-tron Prices, Reviews, and Pictures {{!}} Edmunds|url=https://www.edmunds.com/audi/e-tron/|website=[[Edmunds.com]]}}</ref>

=== Q4 e-tron Sportback (2021) ===
[[File:Audi Q4 Sportback e-tron IAA 2021 1X7A0158.jpg|thumb|Audi Q4 e-tron Sportback is based on the MEB platform.]]

The Audi Q4 e-tron Sportback is a battery electric [[Crossover (automobile)|coupe crossover]] unveiled alongside the standard Q4 e-tron. It will be coming for sale in 2021 along with the Q4 e-tron. Both of these cars will be based on the Volkswagen's [[Volkswagen Group MEB platform|MEB]] platform. The Q4 e-tron Sportback has a drag coefficient of 0.26.<ref name="Perez">{{Cite web|url=https://www.motor1.com/news/432743/audi-q4-e-tron-sportback-concept-details/|title=Audi Q4 E-Tron Sportback Concept Debuts With Sleek Looks, 301 HP|work=[[Motor1]]|last=Perez|first=Jeff|date=7 July 2020|access-date=7 July 2020}}</ref>

=== Q5 e-tron (2021) ===
{{main|Audi Q5 e-tron}}
[[File:2022 SAIC-Audi Q5 e-tron.jpg|thumb|Audi Q5 e-tron is based on MEB platform.]]
The Q5 e-tron is a battery electric [[Crossover (automobile)|crossover SUV]] unveiled at the 2021 [[Guangzhou Auto Show]]. It is based on the MEB platform similar to the Q4 e-tron. Produced and marketed exclusively for the Chinese market, it is the first three-row SUV of the e-tron series.
