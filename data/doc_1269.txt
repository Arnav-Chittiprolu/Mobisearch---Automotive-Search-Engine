The chassis includes braking, suspension, and cooling systems with a battery in the center.<ref name=WGLT_C>{{cite web|url=https://www.wglt.org/post/rivian-reveals-Skateboard-chassis-michigan-conference|title=Rivian Reveals 'Skateboard' Chassis At Michigan Conference|first=Ryan|last=Denham|website= WGLT.org|access-date=April 18, 2019| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20190418053225/https://www.wglt.org/post/rivian-reveals-Skateboard-chassis-michigan-conference|archive-date=April 18, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>
Rivian's relatively flat, low center of gravity chassis is a typical electric vehicle [[Skateboard (automotive platform)|skateboard]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pantagraph.com/business/inside-rivians-test-drive-operation-in-normal/collection_b01efe1d-4372-54b6-a06e-6eb6637b473b.html#15|title=Inside Rivian's test drive operation in Normal|first=David|last=Proeber|date=October 31, 2021|access-date=2025-01-02|website=The Pantagraph}}</ref> which enables straightforward modification by the manufacturer for other body types.<ref name=bloomberg20191223>{{cite news |url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2019-12-23/electric-truck-maker-rivian-raises-1-3-billion-led-by-t-rowe |title=Electric-Truck Maker Rivian Raises $1.3 Billion in New Funding |work=[[Bloomberg News]] |date=December 23, 2019 |access-date=December 24, 2019 |archive-date=December 24, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191224142815/https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2019-12-23/electric-truck-maker-rivian-raises-1-3-billion-led-by-t-rowe |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name=WGLT_C /> The suspension uses a "sophisticated suspension architecture" with [[dynamic roll control]].<ref name=insideevs20210928/> The [[air suspension]] allows adjustment of the ride height through a range of {{cvt|6.5|in|mm}}, giving the R1T a variable [[ground clearance]] of {{cvt|7.9â€“14.4|in|mm}}. Adaptive dampers are used for all four wheels; the front uses unequal-length [[Double wishbone suspension|double-wishbone suspension]] while the rear uses a [[multi-link suspension]].

The front-to-rear weight distribution is 51/49.<ref name=mtrend20210928/> Overall, the vehicle is {{cvt|217.1|in|m}} long, {{cvt|81.8|in|m}} wide (with mirrors folded), and {{cvt|72.1â€“78.3|in|m}} tall.<ref name=ars20210928>{{cite news |title=To the mountain and back: Rivian's electric truck and its 314-mile range |last=Gitlin |first=Jonathan |work=[[Ars Technica]] |date=September 28, 2021 |url=https://arstechnica.com/cars/2021/09/rivians-67-500-314-mile-electric-adventure-truck-put-to-the-test/ |access-date=September 30, 2021 |archive-date=September 29, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210929130825/https://arstechnica.com/cars/2021/09/rivians-67-500-314-mile-electric-adventure-truck-put-to-the-test/ |url-status=live }}</ref> The [[gross vehicle weight rating]] is {{cvt|8532|lb|kg}} when carrying its maximum load of {{cvt|1700|lb|kg}} (driver + cargo + passengers).<ref name=ars20210928/> The [[coefficient of drag]] is 0.30.<ref>{{cite web |title=2022 Rivian R1T (Launch Edition (135 kWh)) Specs |url=https://www.motormatchup.com/catalog/Rivian/R1T/2022/Launch-Edition-(135-kWh) |website=Motor Matchup |access-date=April 18, 2022}}</ref>

Rivian has applied for a patent on a novel tight-turning technique where [[Differential steering|differential motor torque]] between the turned front wheels and back wheels is applied.<ref name=gcr20210709>{{cite news |title=Rivian patents another maneuverability trick enabled by quad-motor layout |url=https://www.greencarreports.com/news/1132844_rivian-patents-another-maneuverability-trick-enabled-by-quad-motor-layout |date=July 9, 2021 |access-date=August 7, 2021 |work=Green Car Reports |archive-date=August 7, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210807155553/https://www.greencarreports.com/news/1132844_rivian-patents-another-maneuverability-trick-enabled-by-quad-motor-layout |url-status=live }}</ref>  Previously referred to as "Tank Turn" mode, the software to support the feature was not ready and so it was not included with the initial production trucks in late 2021.<ref name=insideevs20210928/> In June 2023, Rivian announced it would not release the feature, as using it could damage roadways and surfaces.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.autonews.com/design/why-rivian-canceled-highly-touted-tank-turn-feature |title=Rivian cancels 'tank turn' feature to prevent trail abuse |author=Iliff, Laurence |date=June 1, 2023 |work=Automotive News |access-date=June 2, 2023 |url-access=subscription}}</ref> Edit summary (Briefly describe your changes) By publishing changes, you agree to the Terms of Use , and you irrevocably agree to release your contribution under the CC BY-SA 4.0 License and the GFDL .
