In the US, about 34,000 people were killed by traffic crashes every year from 2015 to 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www-fars.nhtsa.dot.gov/Main/index.aspx |title=FATALITY ANALYSIS REPORTING SYSTEM (FARS) ENCYCLOPEDIA |publisher=National Highway Traffic Safety Administration |date=2016-01-29 |access-date=2022-09-29}}</ref> Sharp rises in the price of fuel and related driver behavioural changes reduced 2007-8 highway fatalities in the US to below the 1961 fatality count.<ref name=Sivak08>{{Cite journal|title=Is the U.S. on the Path to the Lowest Motor Vehicle Fatalities in Decades?|journal=Umtri-2008-39|date=July 2008|first=Michael|last=Sivak |hdl=2027.42/60424 }}</ref> Litigation played a large part in mandating safer cars.<ref>{{cite journal |doi=10.1525/can.2004.19.1.61 |title="Dangerous Instrumentality": The Bystander as Subject in Automobility |journal=Cultural Anthropology |volume=19 |pages=61â€“94 |year=2004 |last1=Jain |first1=Sarah S. Lochlann |s2cid=17924196 |doi-access=free }}</ref>

Safety is a significant concern in the European Union (EU 28) with 25,249<ref name="etsc.eu">{{Cite web|url=https://etsc.eu/13th-annual-road-safety-performance-index-pin-report/|title=13th Annual Road Safety Performance Index (PIN) Report {{!}} ETSC|website=etsc.eu|date=18 June 2019 |access-date=2019-07-04}}</ref> fatalities and around 130,000<ref>{{Cite press release|url=http://europa.eu/rapid/press-release_IP-19-1951_en.htm|title= Road safety: Data show improvements in 2018 but further concrete and swift actions are needed|website=European Commission|access-date=2019-07-04}}</ref> serious injuries in 2018 and 2017 respectively. Overall, between 2001 and 2018, there was an almost 50% reduction in road deaths in the EU from 55,092 in 2001 to the 2018 figure of 25,249.<ref name="etsc.eu"/> In 2018, the EU average road death rate was 49 people per million inhabitants.<ref name="etsc.eu"/> Also in 2018, Romania had the worst figures in this regard with 96 people out of a million inhabitants dying on the roads, Belgium had the median score standing at 52 deaths per million, and the UK scored the best with 28 people out of every million dying on its roads.<ref name="etsc.eu"/>

===International comparison===
In 1996, the US had about 2 deaths per 10,000 motor vehicles, compared to 1.9 in Germany, 2.6 in France, and 1.5 in the UK.<ref name="factbook">{{cite web|url=http://www.factbook.net/EGRF_Regional_analyses_HMCs.htm |title=Estimating global road fatalities - Regional Analyses - Highly Motorised Countries |access-date=2014-12-12 |url-status=usurped |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100704165123/http://www.factbook.net/EGRF_Regional_analyses_HMCs.htm |archive-date=July 4, 2010 }}</ref> In 1998, there were 3,421 fatal crashes in the UK, the fewest since 1926;<ref>[https://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk/442834.stm "UK Road deaths fall to record low"], BBC News, September 9, 1999.</ref> in 2010, this number was further reduced to 1,857 and was attributed to the 2009â€“2010 [[scrappage scheme]].<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.autoexpress.co.uk/news/autoexpressnews/270052/scrappage_cuts_road_death_rate.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110718021822/http://www.autoexpress.co.uk/news/autoexpressnews/270052/scrappage_cuts_road_death_rate.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=2011-07-18 |title=Scrappage cuts road death rate &#124; News |magazine=Auto Express |access-date=2011-09-20}}</ref>

===US specificities===
[[Transportation safety in the United States]] is monitored by various agencies.

[[File:1994- Motor vehicle traffic deaths in road accidents, by country.svg |thumb|Unlike most other developed nations, per capita road accident deaths in the US reversed their decline in the early 2010s.<ref name=OECDaccidents_20231215>{{cite web |title=OECD Data / Road Accidents |url=https://data.oecd.org/transport/road-accidents.htm |website=data.OECD.org |publisher=Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230706145327/https://data.oecd.org/transport/road-accidents.htm |archive-date=6 July 2023 |date=15 December 2023 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name=NYTimes_20231211>{{cite news |last1=Leonhardt |first1=David |title=The Rise in U.S. Traffic Deaths / What's behind America's unique problem with vehicle crashes? |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2023/12/11/briefing/us-traffic-deaths.html |work=The New York Times |date=11 December 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231214145010/https://www.nytimes.com/2023/12/11/briefing/us-traffic-deaths.html |archive-date=14 December 2023 |url-status=live }}</ref>]]

Research on the trends in use of heavy vehicles indicate that a significant difference between the US and other countries is the relatively high prevalence of [[pickup truck]]s and [[sport utility vehicle|SUV]]s in the US. A 2003 study by the US [[Transportation Research Board]] found that SUVs and pickup trucks are significantly less safe than passenger cars, that non-US branded vehicles tend to be safer than US branded vehicles, and that the size and weight of a vehicle has a significantly smaller effect on safety than the quality of the vehicle's engineering.<ref>{{Cite web
