====Dual-Motor====
In March 2022, Rivian announced it would develop and sell a dual-motor powertrain for the R1T, with one electric motor for each axle. The price of the dual-motor (DM) version would start around the same initial price at which the quad-motor (QM) version was offered, while the QM version would increase in price by almost 20%. The output of the DM version was expected to be more than {{cvt|600|hp}} and {{cvt|600|lbft}} of torque,<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.cnet.com/roadshow/news/rivian-dual-motor-r1s-r1t-launch-quad-motor-price-increase/ |title=Rivian Launches 600-HP Dual-Motor R1S and R1T, Raises Quad-Motor Pricing |author=Golson, Daniel |date=March 1, 2022 |work=Road/Show |publisher=CNet |access-date=May 26, 2023}}</ref> but was later published by Rivian at {{cvt|533|hp}} and {{cvt|610|lbft}} of torque.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://stories.rivian.com/difference-between-r1-drive-systems | title=Rivian Drive Systems, Decoded - Rivian Stories }}</ref> The largest ("Max") battery was removed as an option for the QM version in December, limiting availability to DM versions only.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://insideevs.com/news/627793/rivian-r1t-r1s-no-quad-motor-max-pack-2023/ |title=Rivian Removes Variant Of Its R1T For 2023, Provides Other Updates |author=Loveday, Steven |date=December 21, 2022 |work=Inside EVs |access-date=May 26, 2023}}</ref> The "Enduro" traction motors for the DM versions were developed by Rivian in-house and reduce the number of semiconductors used compared to the QM, alleviating a key component supply restraint;<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.carscoops.com/2023/04/rivian-is-making-its-own-new-motors-to-speed-up-production-and-reduce-delays/ |title=Rivian Is Making Its Own New Motors To Speed Up Production And Reduce Delays |author=Bell, Sebastien |date=April 14, 2023 |work=CarScoops |access-date=May 30, 2023}}</ref> production began in February 2023,<ref name=IEVs-20230224>{{cite news |url=https://insideevs.com/news/654215/rivian-starts-production-enduro-motors-for-dual-motor-awd-models/ |title=Rivian Starts Production Of Enduro Motors For Dual-Motor AWD Models |author=Mihalascu, Dan |date=February 24, 2023 |work=Inside EVs |access-date=May 26, 2023}}</ref> although most of the initial production were diverted to [[Rivian EDV]]s.

In April 2023, Rivian introduced a "Performance Dual-Motor" option (PDM), which uses software to unlock enhanced performance from the DM motors. With the PDM option, the R1T will produce approximately {{cvt|700|hp}} and {{cvt|700|lbft}} of torque.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.theverge.com/2023/4/7/23673961/rivian-performance-dual-motor-availability-max-pack-battery |title=Rivian's new 'Performance' powertrain lets R1T reservation holders keep their big battery |author=Shakir, Umar |date=April 7, 2023 |work=The Verge |access-date=May 26, 2023}}</ref> As tested by ''Motor Trend'', each PDM [[traction motor]] has a maximum output of {{cvt|350|hp}} and {{cvt|428|lbft}}; combined output is slightly less than double, at {{cvt|665|hp}} and {{cvt|829|lbft}}. Externally, the PDM (silver accented badging, black brake calipers) can be distinguished from the QM (yellow accented badging and yellow brake calipers).<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.motortrend.com/reviews/2023-rivian-r1t-dual-motor-performance-first-test-review/ |title=2023 Rivian R1T Dual-Motor Performance First Test: It's the Same Picture |author=Evans, Scott |author2=Walker, William (photographer) |date=July 13, 2023 |work=Motor Trend |access-date=July 14, 2023}}</ref>

The first R1T DM was completed on May 25, 2023, with deliveries to commence in June.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://stories.rivian.com/dual-motor-r1t-start-of-production |title=First Customer Dual-Motor R1T rolls off the line |date=May 25, 2023 |publisher=Rivian Automotive |access-date=May 26, 2023}}</ref>

====Storage batteries====
The Rivian R1T was initially offered with three different batteries sized 105 kWh ("Standard"), 135 kWh ("Large"), and 180 kWh ("Max") of energy storage.<ref name=motortrend20210910>{{cite news |last=Lin |first=Kelly |date=September 10, 2021 |title=2022 Rivian R1Tk |url=https://www.motortrend.com/cars/rivian/r1t/ |access-date=September 13, 2021 |work=[[Motor Trend]] |archive-date=September 13, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210913204921/https://www.motortrend.com/cars/rivian/r1t/ |url-status=live }}</ref> Rivian R1T has an estimated range of {{cvt|230|mi}} with the smallest battery, {{cvt|314|mi}} for the medium battery, and over {{cvt|400|mi}} for the largest battery.<ref name=mtrend20210928/> Additional batteries can be mounted in the R1T's bed for improved range. Those backup/auxiliary batteries can be charged by another R1T, if no charging infrastructure is available.<ref name=motorauthority20210917>{{cite news |title=Rivians will be able to charge each other, auxiliary battery packs confirmed |url=https://www.motorauthority.com/news/1123515_rivians-will-be-able-to-charge-each-other-auxiliary-battery-packs-confirmed |date=September 17, 2021 |access-date=September 17, 2021 |work=Motor Authority |archive-date=September 17, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210917021451/https://www.motorauthority.com/news/1123515_rivians-will-be-able-to-charge-each-other-auxiliary-battery-packs-confirmed |url-status=live }}</ref>

The "Large" battery pack consists of 7,776 cells ([[2170 battery|2170 type]]<ref name=insideevs20210928/><!-- @17:10 in the video; is not mentioned in the article text -->) in a nine-module assembly for the 135 kWh battery pack.<ref name=mtrend20210928/> The [[Li-ion]]<ref name=ars20210928/> cells are manufactured by [[Samsung]]; battery pack is assembled by Rivian in Normal, Illinois.<ref name=electrek20210928/> A flashlight is available on the driver-side door containing a Li-ion cell identical to the cells used in the main battery, bringing the total cell count in the vehicle to 7,777.<ref name=tdrive2021>{{cite news |last=Gilboy |first=James |title=2022 Rivian R1T First Drive Review: The Electric Pickup Revolution Is Real, and It's Here |url=https://www.thedrive.com/new-cars/42512/2022-rivian-r1t-first-drive-review-the-electric-pickup-revolution-is-real-and-its-here |date=September 28, 2021 |access-date=October 1, 2021 |work=The Drive |archive-date=September 30, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210930173842/https://www.thedrive.com/new-cars/42512/2022-rivian-r1t-first-drive-review-the-electric-pickup-revolution-is-real-and-its-here |url-status=live }}</ref>

The "Standard" battery pack will use {{chem|Li|Fe|P|O|4|link=Lithium iron phosphate}} chemistry, which eliminates the use of nickel and cobalt, reducing costs.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.cnbc.com/2022/03/10/rivian-changing-battery-cells-to-lfp-following-tesla-and-high-nickel.html |title=Rivian will follow Tesla and change the type of battery cells it uses in standard packs |author=Kolodny, Lora |date=March 10, 2022 |work=CNBC |access-date=May 26, 2023}}</ref> R1T vehicles with "Standard" batteries are expected to ship in 2023.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.thedrive.com/news/rivian-battery-switch-could-increase-production-but-lower-range |title=Rivian Battery Switch Could Increase Production but Lower Range |author=Holderith, Peter |date=April 6, 2023 |work=The Drive |access-date=May 26, 2023}}</ref>{{Needs update|date=October 2025}}

====Efficiency and range====
The U.S. EPA rates the 2022 model year R1T QM at {{cvt|70|mpge}} for the combined city/highway [[driving cycle]], tying it with the [[Ford F-150 Lightning]] as the most efficient "Standard Pickup Truck" sold for that model year. City economy is slightly better at {{cvt|74|mpge}} than the rated highway economy of {{cvt|66|mpge}}.<ref>{{Fuel Economy Guide |year=2022 |page=9;25}}</ref> According to ''[[Car and Driver]]'' magazine, the real world range with the "Large" 129 kWh battery ranges from {{convert|220|to|280|miles|km|round=5|abbr=out}} highway driving, depending on tire choice.<ref>{{Cite web |date=March 3, 2022 |title=2022 Rivian R1T Review, Pricing, and Specs |url=https://www.caranddriver.com/rivian/r1t |access-date=June 16, 2022 |website=Car and Driver |language=en-us}}</ref> Wheel size also affects estimated range.<ref name=IEVs-20230408>{{cite news |url=https://insideevs.com/news/661401/rivian-r1t-performance-dual-motor-max-pack-range/ |title=Rivian Adds R1T Performance Dual-Motor Option, Details Max Pack Battery Ranges |author=Dnistran, Iulian |date=April 8, 2023 |work=Inside EVs |access-date=May 26, 2023}}</ref>

Regenerative braking is possible at up to 0.21 [[g-force|g]], which charges the battery with energy dissipated during braking.<ref name=electrek20210928/> The R1T can charge at up to 225&nbsp;kW and supports [[ISO 15118|Plug&Charge]].<ref name=electrek20210928>{{cite news |title=Rivian R1T first drive: Easily the best pickup I've ever driven, both off road and on |last=Weintraub |first=Seth |work=Electrek |date=September 28, 2021 |url=https://electrek.co/2021/09/28/rivian-r1t-first-drive-easily-the-best-pickup-ive-ever-driven-both-off-road-and-on/ |access-date=September 29, 2021 |archive-date=September 28, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210928211143/https://electrek.co/2021/09/28/rivian-r1t-first-drive-easily-the-best-pickup-ive-ever-driven-both-off-road-and-on/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

===Chassis===
The chassis includes braking, suspension, and cooling systems with a battery in the center.<ref name=WGLT_C>{{cite web|url=https://www.wglt.org/post/rivian-reveals-Skateboard-chassis-michigan-conference|title=Rivian Reveals 'Skateboard' Chassis At Michigan Conference|first=Ryan|last=Denham|website= WGLT.org|access-date=April 18, 2019| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20190418053225/https://www.wglt.org/post/rivian-reveals-Skateboard-chassis-michigan-conference|archive-date=April 18, 2019|url-status=live}}</ref>
Rivian's relatively flat, low center of gravity chassis is a typical electric vehicle [[Skateboard (automotive platform)|skateboard]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pantagraph.com/business/inside-rivians-test-drive-operation-in-normal/collection_b01efe1d-4372-54b6-a06e-6eb6637b473b.html#15|title=Inside Rivian's test drive operation in Normal|first=David|last=Proeber|date=October 31, 2021|access-date=2025-01-02|website=The Pantagraph}}</ref> which enables straightforward modification by the manufacturer for other body types.<ref name=bloomberg20191223>{{cite news |url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2019-12-23/electric-truck-maker-rivian-raises-1-3-billion-led-by-t-rowe |title=Electric-Truck Maker Rivian Raises $1.3 Billion in New Funding |work=[[Bloomberg News]] |date=December 23, 2019 |access-date=December 24, 2019 |archive-date=December 24, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191224142815/https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2019-12-23/electric-truck-maker-rivian-raises-1-3-billion-led-by-t-rowe |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name=WGLT_C /> The suspension uses a "sophisticated suspension architecture" with [[dynamic roll control]].<ref name=insideevs20210928/> The [[air suspension]] allows adjustment of the ride height through a range of {{cvt|6.5|in|mm}}, giving the R1T a variable [[ground clearance]] of {{cvt|7.9â€“14.4|in|mm}}. Adaptive dampers are used for all four wheels; the front uses unequal-length [[Double wishbone suspension|double-wishbone suspension]] while the rear uses a [[multi-link suspension]].

The front-to-rear weight distribution is 51/49.<ref name=mtrend20210928/> Overall, the vehicle is {{cvt|217.1|in|m}} long, {{cvt|81.8|in|m}} wide (with mirrors folded), and {{cvt|72.1â€“78.3|in|m}} tall.<ref name=ars20210928>{{cite news |title=To the mountain and back: Rivian's electric truck and its 314-mile range |last=Gitlin |first=Jonathan |work=[[Ars Technica]] |date=September 28, 2021 |url=https://arstechnica.com/cars/2021/09/rivians-67-500-314-mile-electric-adventure-truck-put-to-the-test/ |access-date=September 30, 2021 |archive-date=September 29, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210929130825/https://arstechnica.com/cars/2021/09/rivians-67-500-314-mile-electric-adventure-truck-put-to-the-test/ |url-status=live }}</ref> The [[gross vehicle weight rating]] is {{cvt|8532|lb|kg}} when carrying its maximum load of {{cvt|1700|lb|kg}} (driver + cargo + passengers).<ref name=ars20210928/> The [[coefficient of drag]] is 0.30.<ref>{{cite web |title=2022 Rivian R1T (Launch Edition (135 kWh)) Specs |url=https://www.motormatchup.com/catalog/Rivian/R1T/2022/Launch-Edition-(135-kWh) |website=Motor Matchup |access-date=April 18, 2022}}</ref>

Rivian has applied for a patent on a novel tight-turning technique where [[Differential steering|differential motor torque]] between the turned front wheels and back wheels is applied.<ref name=gcr20210709>{{cite news |title=Rivian patents another maneuverability trick enabled by quad-motor layout |url=https://www.greencarreports.com/news/1132844_rivian-patents-another-maneuverability-trick-enabled-by-quad-motor-layout |date=July 9, 2021 |access-date=August 7, 2021 |work=Green Car Reports |archive-date=August 7, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210807155553/https://www.greencarreports.com/news/1132844_rivian-patents-another-maneuverability-trick-enabled-by-quad-motor-layout |url-status=live }}</ref>  Previously referred to as "Tank Turn" mode, the software to support the feature was not ready and so it was not included with the initial production trucks in late 2021.<ref name=insideevs20210928/> In June 2023, Rivian announced it would not release the feature, as using it could damage roadways and surfaces.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.autonews.com/design/why-rivian-canceled-highly-touted-tank-turn-feature |title=Rivian cancels 'tank turn' feature to prevent trail abuse |author=Iliff, Laurence |date=June 1, 2023 |work=Automotive News |access-date=June 2, 2023 |url-access=subscription}}</ref>

===Performance===
The R1T QM has a tested acceleration time of 3.0 seconds from 0 to {{cvt|60|mph}}.<ref name=motortrend20210910/><ref>{{Cite web |date=March 18, 2022 |title=Here Are All The Electric Pickup Trucks Arriving In 2022 And Beyond |url=https://www.rategenius.com/electric-pickup-trucks-2022 |access-date=June 16, 2022 |website=RateGenius |language=en-US}}</ref> Top speed is {{cvt|110|mph|km/h}}.<ref name=carbuzz20210928/>

===Interior features===
The R1T has four electrical outlets: dual 15A/120V AC outlets in the cargo bed; single 15A/120V AC outlet in the [[Trunk (car)#Designs|frunk]] and another in the gear tunnel.<ref name=ars20210928/>

The interior [[display size]] is {{cvt|12|in|cm}} for the driver's instrument cluster plus a {{cvt|15|in|cm}} combined display to control infotainment, navigation, climate, comfort functions in the center of dashboard.<ref name=carbuzz20210928>{{cite news |last=Lingeman |first=Jake |title=2022 Rivian R1T Truck: Review, Trims, Specs, Price, New Interior Features, Exterior Design, and Specifications |work=CarBuzz |date=September 28, 2021 |url=https://carbuzz.com/cars/rivian/r1t |access-date=October 3, 2021 }}</ref>

=== Hands-free driving ===
It is equipped with 11 cameras, 12 [[ultrasonic sensors]], and 5 radar sensors which will be used in Rivian's suite of [[advanced driver assistance systems]].<ref name=mtrend20210928/>

R1T has safety and driver assistance package called the Driver+. On selected highways the truck is capable of self-steering and controlling speed following the instructions of the driver similar to [[Tesla Autopilot|Tesla's AutoPilot]]. During hands-free driving the driver has to keep their eyes on road and Rivian R1T has cameras inside the cabin to monitor the driver's activity.<ref>{{Cite web |title=R1T - Rivian |url=https://rivian.com/r1t |access-date=June 16, 2022 |website=rivian.com |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Gastelu |first=Gary |date=October 6, 2021 |title=Elon Musk laughs at Rivian's 'autonomous' driving feature |url=https://www.foxbusiness.com/lifestyle/elon-musk-laughs-rivians-autonomous-driving-feature |access-date=June 16, 2022 |website=FOXBusiness |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Rivian is making its hands-free driver assistance system standard in 'every vehicle' it builds |url=https://techcrunch.com/2020/11/11/rivian-is-making-its-hands-free-driver-assistance-system-standard-in-every-vehicle-it-builds/ |access-date=June 16, 2022 |website=TechCrunch |language=en-US}}</ref> The hands-free feature works well in straight roads but needs driver's input when taking corners.<ref>{{Cite web |date=December 15, 2021 |title=Rivian Driver+ Vs Ford BlueCruise Vs Tesla AutoPilot - Car, Truck And Vehicle How To Guides - Vehicle Freak |url=https://vehiclefreak.com/rivian-driver-vs-ford-bluecruise-vs-tesla-autopilot/ |access-date=June 16, 2022 |language=en-US}}</ref>

=== Wiring ===
Second generation R1S/T vehicles adopted a zonal wiring architecture or the earlier ECU/device approach. The new approach reduces the total wiring length by 1.6 miles and weight by {{Convert|20|kg|abbr=on}}, reducing material costs by 20% and carbon footprint  by 15%.

The earlier R1s used a domain-based architecture, pairing each category of software with a piece of hardware. The result required 17 ECUs. The second generation provides four systems with separate ECUs: infotainment, autonomy, vehicle access, drive units, and battery management. The remaining functions require only three ECUs.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Shaw |first=Kristin |date=August 6, 2024 |title=How Rivian reduced electrical wiring by 1.6 miles and 44 pounds |url=https://www.popsci.com/technology/rivian-zonal-electrical-architecture/ |access-date=August 8, 2024 |website=Popular Science |language=en-US}}</ref>{{clarify|reason=4 systems with 5 ECUs - plus another 3 ECUs.
