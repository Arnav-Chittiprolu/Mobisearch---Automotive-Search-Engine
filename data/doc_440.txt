From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia Type of motorcycle BMW HP2 Enduro Manufacturer BMW Motorrad Production 2005–2008 Class Dual Sport Engine 1,170 cc air/oil cooled boxer twin Power 105 hp (78 kW) @ 7,000 rpm [ 1 ] Torque 75 foot-pounds (102 N⋅m) @ 5,500 rpm [ 1 ] Frame type Trellis tube frame Suspension 45 mm telescoping fork Weight 386 lb (175 kg) [ 1 ] ( dry ) Related R1200GS , HP2 Megamoto, HP2 Sport The HP2 Enduro is a motorcycle manufactured by BMW Motorrad from 2005 to 2008. [ 2 ] [ 3 ] It is a "High Performance" (HP) dirt bike based on the engine and electronics from the R1200GS adventure-touring model. [ 1 ] The use of a trellis tube frame , a conventional fork rather than Telelever , and other modifications, make it 53 lb (24 kg) lighter than the corresponding R1200GS. [ 1 ] The HP2 Enduro was discontinued in 2008. [ 4 ] Approximately 2910 HP2 Enduro's were produced in total: 2517 Euro Spec bikes and 393 US spec bikes.
