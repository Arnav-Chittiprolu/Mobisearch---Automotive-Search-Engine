[[QuantumScape]] is a solid-state battery startup that spun out of [[Stanford University]]. It went public on the [[New York Stock Exchange|NYSE]] on November 29, 2020, as part of a [[Special-purpose acquisition company|SPAC]] merger with Kensington Capital.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Wayland |first=Michael |date=2020-09-03 |title=Bill Gates-backed vehicle battery supplier to go public through SPAC deal |url=https://www.cnbc.com/2020/09/03/bill-gates-backed-ev-battery-supplier-to-go-public-through-spac-deal.html |access-date=2021-01-07 |website=CNBC |language=en}}</ref><ref name="Manchester20201130">{{cite news |last1=Manchester |first1=Bette |date=30 November 2020 |title=QuantumScape successfully goes public |work=electrive.com |url=https://www.electrive.com/2020/11/30/quantumscape-successfully-goes-public/}}</ref> In 2022 the company introduced its 24-layer A0 prototype cells. In Q1 2023, it introduced QSE-5, a 5 amp-hour lithium metal cell. Volkswagen's PowerCo stated that the A0 prototype had met the announced performance metrics. QuantumScape's FlexFrame design combines prismatic and pouch cell designs to accommodate the expansion and contraction of its cells during cycling.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Doll |first=Scooter |date=January 16, 2024 |title=QuantumScape unveils beating heart of its solid-state battery tech: the FlexFrame cell |url=https://electrek.co/2024/01/16/quantumscape-unveils-beating-heart-solid-state-battery-tech-flexframe-cell-video/ |work=Electrek.co}}</ref><ref>{{Citation |last=QuantumScape |title=Introducing FlexFrame, QuantumScape's Proprietary Cell Format (video) |website=Youtube |date=16 January 2024 |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Zt49j3yz4PQ |access-date=2024-02-09 }}</ref>

In July 2021, [[Murata Manufacturing]] announced that it would begin mass production, targeting manufacturers of earphones and other wearables.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Fukutomi |first1=Shuntaro |title=Murata to mass-produce all-solid-state batteries in fall |url=https://asia.nikkei.com/Business/Technology/Murata-to-mass-produce-all-solid-state-batteries-in-fall2 |website=Nikkei Asia |access-date=19 July 2021}}</ref> Cell capacity is up to 25&nbsp;mAh at 3.8&nbsp;V,<ref>{{cite web|title=Murata develops solid state battery for wearables applications|work=Murata Manufacturing Co., Ltd. |url=https://www.murata.com/en-us/news/batteries/solid_state/2019/0626|date=29 July 2021}}</ref> making it suitable for small mobile devices such as earbuds, but not for electric vehicles. Lithium-ion cells used in electric vehicles typically offer 2,000 to 5,000&nbsp;mAh at a similar voltage:<ref>{{cite web|title=Category: 18650/20700/21700 Rechargeable batteries|url=https://www.batteryspace.com/18650seriesli-ioncells.aspx|date=29 July 2021}}</ref> an EV would need at least 100 times as many of the Murata cells to provide equivalent power.

Ford Motor Company and [[BMW]] funded the startup Solid Power with $130 million, and as of 2022 the company had raised $540 million.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2022/05/18/solid-state-batteries-electric-vehicles-race/ |title=Inside the race for a car battery that charges fast â€” and won't catch fire |first=Pranshu |last=Verma |date=18 May 2022 |newspaper=[[The Washington Post]] }}</ref>

In September 2021, [[Toyota]] announced their plan to use a solid-state battery, starting with hybrid models in 2025.<ref>{{cite web|title=Toyota Outlines Solid-State Battery Tech|url=https://www.motortrend.com/news/toyota-battery-bev-hev-solid-state-future-investment/|date=8 September 2021|access-date=12 November 2021}}</ref>

In February 2021, [[Hitachi Zosen Corporation|Hitachi Zosen]] announced demonstration experiments on the [[International Space Station]]. The [[Cygnus (spacecraft)|Cygnus]] No. 17, launched on February 19, 2022, confirmed that all-solid-state batteries would be tested on the ISS.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Deguchi |first=Hayabusa |date=2022-02-27 |title=ãƒãƒ¼ã‚¹ãƒ­ãƒƒãƒ—ãƒ»ã‚°ãƒ©ãƒãƒ³ã€Œã‚·ã‚°ãƒŠã‚¹è£œçµ¦èˆ¹ã€æ‰“ã¡ä¸Šã’æˆåŠŸ æ—¥æœ¬ã®å®Ÿé¨“æ©Ÿå™¨ãƒ»è¶…å°å‹è¡›æ˜Ÿã‚‚æ­è¼‰ |url=https://sorae.info/space/20220227-cygnus.html |access-date=2023-11-22 |website=sorae å®‡å®™ã¸ã®ãƒãƒ¼ã‚¿ãƒ«ã‚µã‚¤ãƒˆ |language=ja}}</ref>

In January 2022, [[ProLogium]] signed a technical cooperation agreement with [[Mercedes-Benz Group|Mercedes-Benz]]. The investment will be used for solid-state battery development and production preparation.<ref name="Taiwan battery maker ProLogium signs investment deal with Mercedes-Benz">{{cite news |date=27 January 2022 |title=Taiwan battery maker ProLogium signs investment deal with Mercedes-Benz |publisher=Reuters |url=https://www.reuters.com/markets/deals/taiwan-battery-maker-prologium-signs-investment-deal-with-mercedes-benz-2022-01-27/ |access-date=1 November 2022 |ref=Taiwan battery maker ProLogium signs investment deal with Mercedes-Benz}}</ref>

In early 2022, Swiss Clean Battery (SCB) announced plans to open the world's first factory for sustainable solid-state batteries in [[Frauenfeld]] by 2024 with an initial annual production of 1.2 GWh.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Swiss Clean Battery plans 7.6-GWh gigafactory |url=https://renewablesnow.com/news/swiss-clean-battery-plans-76-gwh-gigafactory-780244/ |access-date=2023-04-27 |website=Renewables Now |date=8 April 2022 }}</ref>

In July 2022, Svolt announced the production of a 20&nbsp;Ah electric battery with an [[energy density]] of 350-400 Wh/kg.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://cnevpost.com/2022/07/19/svolt-energy-develops-20ah-solid-state-battery-cells/|title=Svolt Energy develops solid-state battery cells that will allow vehicles to reach over 1,000 km range|date=July 19, 2022}}</ref>

In June 2023, [[Maxell|Maxell Corporation]] began mass production of large-capacity solid-state batteries. This battery has a long life and heat resistance. Production of 200 mAh cylindrical solid-state batteries was to begin in January 2024. Size: diameter 23&nbsp;mm/height 27&nbsp;mm.<ref>{{Cite web |title=ä¼šç¤¾å››å­£å ±ã‚ªãƒ³ãƒ©ã‚¤ãƒ³{{pipe}}æ ªå¼æŠ•è³‡ãƒ»éŠ˜æŸ„ç ”ç©¶ã®ãƒã‚¤ãƒ–ãƒ« |url=https://shikiho.toyokeizai.net/news/0/711422 |access-date=2023-10-28 |website=shikiho.toyokeizai.net}}</ref>

In October 2023, Toyota announced a partnership with [[Idemitsu Kosan]] to produce solid-state batteries for their electric vehicles starting in 2028.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Toyota Inks Deal to Mass Produce Solid State EV Batteries With 932-Mile Range |url=https://www.pcmag.com/news/toyota-inks-deal-to-mass-produce-solid-state-ev-batteries-with-932-mile |access-date=2023-10-24 |website=PCMAG |date=13 October 2023 |language=en}}</ref>

In October 2023 Factorial Energy opened a battery manufacturing facility in [[Methuen, Massachusetts]], and began shipping 100&nbsp;Ah A-samples to automotive partners totaling over 1,000 A-sample cells to Mercedes-Benz. Its technology uses a lithium-metal anode, quasi-solid electrolyte and high-capacity cathode. Its energy density is 391&nbsp;Wh/kg.<ref name=":7">{{Cite web |last=Weiss |first=C. In November 2023, [[GAC Group|Guangzhou Automobile Group]] announced that it would adopt solid-state batteries in 2026. The company also revealed that its battery has achieved 400&nbsp;Wh/kg. Mass production was scheduled to begin in 2025.<ref>{{Cite web |title=ä¸­å›½ãƒ»åºƒæ±½é›†å›£ã€ã€Œå…¨å›ºä½“é›»æ± ã€ã‚’2026å¹´ã«æ­è¼‰ã¸ ãƒ™ãƒ³ãƒãƒ£ãƒ¼æŠ•è³‡ã‚„ã‚¹ãƒ”ãƒ³ã‚ªãƒ•ãªã©å¹…åºƒãå¸ƒçŸ³(æ±æ´‹çµŒæ¸ˆã‚ªãƒ³ãƒ©ã‚¤ãƒ³) |url=https://finance.yahoo.co.jp/news/detail/9b85dffb87653f8a9c89d15d88b694e2d7230155 |access-date=2023-12-05 |website=Yahoo!ãƒ•ã‚¡ã‚¤ãƒŠãƒ³ã‚¹ |language=ja}}</ref>

On December 28, 2023, Hyundai published its patent for an "all-solid-state battery system provided with pressurizing device". The cell is a solid-state battery that maintains constant pressure regardless of charging and discharging rates. The system includes an iso-temperature element.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Johnson |first=Peter |date=January 2, 2024 |title=Hyundai patents an all-solid-state EV battery system in the US |url=https://electrek.co/2024/01/02/hyundai-patent-all-solid-state-ev-battery-system/ |access-date=February 8, 2024 |work=electrek.co}}</ref>

In January 2024, [[Volkswagen]] announced that test results of a prototype solid-state battery retained 95% of its capacity after 1000 charges (equivalent to driving 500,000&nbsp;km). It also passed other performance tests.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Flaherty |first=Nick |date=2024-01-06 |title=VW validates lithium metal solid state battery performance |url=https://www.eenewseurope.com/en/vw-validates-lithium-metal-solid-state-battery-performance/ |access-date=2024-01-08 |website=eeNews Europe |language=en-US}}</ref>

In April 2024, Factorial signed a memorandum of understanding with LG Chem. In June it sent its first 106&nbsp;Ah B-samples to Mercedes-Benz for testing.<ref name=":7" />

In August 2025, ION Storage Systems announces "Cornerstone" Cells with sampling of cells to consumer electronics companies. ION Storage Systems cells are unique as they do not require compression.<ref>{{Cite web |last= |first= |date=August 12, 2025 |title=ION Storage Systems Launches "Cornerstone," Becoming the First Solid State Battery Company to Ship Samples to Consumer Electronics Manufacturers |url=https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/ion-storage-systems-launches-cornerstone-becoming-the-first-solid-state-battery-company-to-ship-samples-to-consumer-electronics-manufacturers-302527774.html |url-status=live |access-date=2025-11-15 |website=www.prnewswire.com |language=en}}</ref>
