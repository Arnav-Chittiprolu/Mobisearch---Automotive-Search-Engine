The '''GMC Hummer EV''' (badged as '''H<sub>EV</sub>''') is a line of [[Battery electric vehicle|battery electric]] heavy-duty vehicles produced by [[General Motors]] since 2021, and sold under the [[GMC (marque)|GMC]] mark.<ref name=Motor1-20200814>{{cite web |url= https://www.motor1.com/news/439168/hummer-ev-logo-trademark/ |title= GMC Hummer EV Logo Trademark Filing Reveals Truck's Possible Emblem |author= Chris Bruce |date= 14 August 2020 |publisher= Motor 1 |access-date= October 23, 2020 |archive-date= October 26, 2020 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20201026184847/https://www.motor1.com/news/439168/hummer-ev-logo-trademark/ |url-status= live }}</ref> The Hummer EV is offered in two variants: a pickup truck and a [[sport utility vehicle]] (SUV), unveiled in October 2020 and April 2021 respectively.<ref name=":0">{{cite web |url = https://www.caranddriver.com/news/a30714182/gmc-hummer-ev-confirmed-2021/ |title = GMC's Hummer EV Pickup Is Real, Will Have 1000 HP |last = Capparella |first = Joey |date = 2020-01-30 |website = Car and Driver |language = en-US |access-date = 2020-01-31 |archive-date = November 1, 2020 |archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20201101154030/https://www.caranddriver.com/news/a30714182/gmc-hummer-ev-confirmed-2021/ |url-status = live }}</ref>

== Overview ==
[[File:2022 Cleveland Auto Show (51939223421).jpg|thumb|left|Hummer EV Truck in Arctic White]]

The Hummer EV is assembled in Detroit and uses batteries produced by [[LG Chem]] in South Korea, with plans later to start producing batteries in the U.S.<ref name="Road and Track 7 April 2022">{{Cite news |last=Ulrich |first=Lawrence |date=April 7, 2022 |title=The Hummer EV Proves Some Things Never Change |work=[[Road and Track]] |url=https://www.roadandtrack.com/reviews/a39653511/hummer-ev-review/ |url-status=live |access-date=April 18, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230418021813/https://www.roadandtrack.com/reviews/a39653511/hummer-ev-review/ |archive-date=April 18, 2023}}</ref> It is planned for SUV models produced from 2024 onward to be equipped to charge other electric vehicles using an onboard 19.2&nbsp;kW charger.<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.thedrive.com/news/2024-gmc-hummer-ev-suv-uses-enormous-battery-to-charge-other-electric-cars |title= 2024 GMC Hummer EV SUV Uses Enormous Battery to Charge Other Electric Cars |last= Cole |first= Aaron |website= thedrive.com |date= March 14, 2023 |access-date= April 19, 2023 |archive-date= April 19, 2023 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20230419183418/https://www.thedrive.com/news/2024-gmc-hummer-ev-suv-uses-enormous-battery-to-charge-other-electric-cars |url-status= live }}</ref>

The electric motors' torque profile make it suitable for towing trailers. It includes various options, such as cameras that allow the driver to see underneath it and [[four-wheel steering]].<ref name="The Guardian 7 April 2022">{{Cite news |url= https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2022/apr/07/electric-hummer-vehicles-climate-crisis |title= I test-drove the all-electric Hummer. The [[pickup truck]] variant has a longer wheelbase than the SUV.<ref>{{Cite web |url= https://www.thedrive.com/car-reviews/2024-gmc-hummer-ev-suv-first-drive-review-specs-price-options-impressions |title= 2024 GMC Hummer EV SUV First Drive Review: Massive, but Misses Its Mark |last= Cole |first= Aaron |website= thedrive.com |date= March 23, 2023 |access-date= April 18, 2023 |archive-date= April 6, 2023 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20230406224453/https://www.thedrive.com/car-reviews/2024-gmc-hummer-ev-suv-first-drive-review-specs-price-options-impressions |url-status= live }}</ref> The pickup variant has a {{convert|5|ft|adj=on}} high bed with a payload capacity of {{convert|1300|lb}} as well as a {{convert|11.3|cuft|L|adj=on}} [[frunk|front trunk]].<ref name="The Drive 7 April 2022">{{cite web |url= https://www.thedrive.com/new-cars/44917/2022-gmc-hummer-ev-first-drive-review-rolling-decadence-that-goes-like-hell |title= 2022 GMC Hummer EV First Drive Review: Rolling Decadence in a Five-Ton Rocket Ship |website= thedrive.com |last= Gilboy |first= James |date= April 7, 2022 |access-date= April 18, 2023 |archive-date= April 6, 2023 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20230406185646/https://www.thedrive.com/new-cars/44917/2022-gmc-hummer-ev-first-drive-review-rolling-decadence-that-goes-like-hell |url-status= live }}</ref> The front trunk on both models is also intended to store the four [[Targa top|removable roof panels]] when needed. It has eighteen cameras installed, as well as front-and-rear pedestrian alerts designed to aid drivers' situational awareness. These accompany numerous other driver-assistance features such as automatic braking and blind-spot warnings.<ref name="The Verge 21 October 2020">{{Cite news |url= https://www.theverge.com/2020/10/20/21525290/hummer-ev-electric-truck-gmc-price-specs-range |title= The Hummer is back as a 350-mile range 'electric supertruck' that can drive diagonally |work= [[The Verge]] |date= October 21, 2020 |access-date= April 17, 2023 |last= Hawkins |first= Andrew J. |archive-date= July 23, 2021 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20210723043445/https://www.theverge.com/2020/10/20/21525290/hummer-ev-electric-truck-gmc-price-specs-range |url-status= live }}</ref><ref name="Inside EVs 28 May 2021">{{Cite web |last=Ruffo |first=Gustavo Henrique |date=May 28, 2021 |title=GMC Hummer EV's Weight And Quickness Raise Safety Concerns |url=https://insideevs.com/news/510334/gmc-hummer-ev-weight-acceleration/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210528185125/https://insideevs.com/news/510334/gmc-hummer-ev-weight-acceleration/ |archive-date=May 28, 2021 |access-date=April 17, 2021 |website=insideevs.com}}</ref>

=== Origins ===
{{Main|Hummer}}
The GMC Hummer EV originated from the discontinued [[Hummer]] brand that was founded by [[AM General]], the manufacturer of the original [[Humvee|HUMVEE light military vehicle]] that was later adapted to civilian spec and sold to the public as the [[Hummer H1]]. In 1999, General Motors purchased the rights to the Hummer name and began marketing it as a full-fledged brand with a lineup that eventually included the [[Hummer H2]] and [[Hummer H3]]. The Hummer brand was discontinued in 2010 following the [[General Motors bankruptcy]].

=== EV<sup>3X</sup> ===
Following the end of production for the Edition 1, GMC introduced the EV3X trim in 2023 as model year 2023 for the Pickup body style, and model year 2024 for the SUV body style. The Pickup version has near identical specifications as the Edition 1 and features three electric motors with an output of 1,000 horsepower, along with an EPA estimated range of 329 miles with the off-road package and 355 miles with the standard 22-inch wheels. The SUV version of the EV3X trim also features three electric motors, outputting 830 horsepower in the SUV configuration, along with EPA estimated range of 298 miles with the off-road package and 314 miles with the standard 22-inch wheels.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Site Maintenance |url=https://news.gmc.com/newsroom.detail.html/Pages/news/us/en/2023/apr/0421-hummer.html |access-date=2024-08-24 |website=news.gmc.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Johnson |first=Erik |date=April 19, 2024 |title=GMC Hummer EV 3X First Test: Stylin' and Profilin' |url=https://www.motortrend.com/reviews/2023-gmc-hummer-ev-3x-electric-truck-first-test-review/ |website=Motor Trend}}</ref>

=== EV<sup>2x</sup> ===
Includes all the features of EV<sup>2</sup>; however, it will also come standard with four-wheel steering, Crab Walk, adaptive air suspension with adaptive ride control, and optional Extract mode. This trim will output an estimated {{convert|625|hp|kW PS|abbr=on}} using a dual motor system, will have an estimated range of at least {{convert|300|mi}}, and is expected to be available in Spring of 2023.<ref name=":1">{{cite web|last=Chang|first=Brittany|title=GMC will be releasing 3 more variants of its 'super truck' Hummer EV over the next 4 years â€” see how they compare|url=https://www.businessinsider.com/gmc-hummer-ev-trims-compared-edition-1-ev3x-ev2x-ev2-2020-10|access-date=2020-10-30|website=Business Insider|archive-date=October 31, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201031170208/https://www.businessinsider.com/gmc-hummer-ev-trims-compared-edition-1-ev3x-ev2x-ev2-2020-10|url-status=live}}</ref>

=== EV<sup>2</sup> ===
This was set to be the base Hummer EV trim which was to include Super Cruise(R), HD surround view, digital key, infinity roof, a multi pro tailgate, and rear drop glass among other standard features. The EV<sup>2</sup> was expected to output an estimated {{convert|625|hp|kW PS|abbr=on}} using a dual motor system, with an estimated range of at least {{convert|250|mi}}. It was expected to be available by Spring 2024.<ref name=":1" />

== Production ==
The Hummer EV is produced at GM's [[Detroit/Hamtramck Assembly]] (also referred to as Factory Zero) plant in [[Michigan]]. Production of the Hummer EV Truck commenced on November 17, 2021, while the Hummer EV SUV started production on January 30, 2023. In October 2020, GM invested $2.2 billion in the plant for the production of these electric vehicles.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web |last=Best |first=Paul |date=2020-10-16 |title=GM makes $2.2 billion investment in electric vehicle plant where HUMMER EV will be produced |url=https://www.foxbusiness.com/lifestyle/gm-electric-vehicle-plant-hummer |access-date=2023-12-21 |website=FOXBusiness |language=en-US}}</ref> As of Q3 2022, 783 units had been delivered to customers.<ref>[https://investor.gm.com/static-files/557a4748-4bff-44df-b514-2ae06afddd96 Archived copy] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221011170902/https://investor.gm.com/static-files/557a4748-4bff-44df-b514-2ae06afddd96 |date=October 11, 2022 }}</ref> For the 2024 model year, GMC introduced a limited "Omega Edition" trim with production of this trim limited to 550 examples for the Pickup body style and 1,108 for the SUV body style.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Lopez |first=Jonathan |title=Here Are The Production Numbers For 2024 GMC Hummer EV Omega Edition Pickup And SUV |date=July 2, 2024 |url=https://gmauthority.com/blog/2024/07/here-are-the-production-numbers-for-2024-gmc-hummer-ev-omega-edition-pickup-and-suv/}}</ref> As of October 2024, 14,039 units have been produced.<ref>{{Cite web |date=November 23, 2024 |title=GMC Hummer EV Sales Numbers |url=https://gmauthority.com/blog/gm/gmc/gmc-hummer-ev/gmc-hummer-ev-sales-numbers/}}</ref> The Hummer EV is manufactured by General Motors exclusively in left-hand drive (LHD) configuration. For right-hand drive (RHD) markets, conversions are carried out by '''Autogroup International''',<ref>{{Cite web |title=2025 GMC Hummer EV Right Hand Drive |url=https://autogroupinternational.com |access-date=2025-09-21 |website=autogroupinternational.com/ |language=en-US}}</ref> a specialist vehicle engineering company.<ref>{{Cite web |title=2025 GMC Hummer EV SUV & Pickup â€“ Right-Hand Drive |url=https://www.gmcaustralia.com/2026-gmc-hummer-ev-suv-and-pickup-right-hand-drive/ |access-date=2025-09-21 |website=GMC Australia |language=en-US}}</ref> These conversions are performed to '''original equipment manufacturer (OEM) standards''', enabling the Hummer EV to be compliant and operable in countries where RHD vehicles are required.<ref>{{Cite web |title=GMC Hummer EV Approved for Australia |url=https://www.carsauce.com/car-news/gmc-hummer-ev-approved-for-australia |access-date=2025-09-21 |website=www.carsauce.com |language=en-AU}}</ref>
