Spouse Meagan McGone ​ ​ ( m. 2014; div. 2025) ​ Children 3 Robert Joseph Scaringe (born January 19, 1983) is an American engineer and businessman who is the founder and chief executive officer (CEO) of electric vehicle manufacturer Rivian . [ 3 ] Early life [ edit ] Robert Joseph Scaringe was born on January 19, 1983. [ 4 ] His father, also named Robert Scaringe, is an engineer and founder of Mainstream Engineering Corporation. [ 5 ] Scaringe grew up a car enthusiast, restoring them in his neighbor's garage. After learning of the great negative environmental effects of cars , however, he decided to focus his life on creating a more sustainable and environmentally friendly transportation system. [ 6 ] Scaringe grew up in Melbourne, Florida . [ 7 ] He attended Melbourne Central Catholic High School . [ 8 ] Scaringe received his bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute . He then received a master's degree in mechanical engineering at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) and went on to also receive a doctorate in mechanical engineering at MIT's Sloan Automotive Lab. [ 9 ] [ 10 ] Career [ edit ] Scaringe returned to Rockledge, Florida , and founded Mainstream Motors in the year of 2009 as the sole employee. The name of the company changed to Rivian Automotive in 2011. [ 11 ] [ 12 ] In November 2021, Rivian went public, listing its shares on the Nasdaq . [ 13 ] Scaringe is chairman of the board and has 9.5% voting power. Additionally, he has veto power over board decisions due to his 100% ownership of the company's class B shares . [ 14 ] In total, Scaringe owns about 1.4% of Rivian. [ 15 ] Personal life [ edit ] Scaringe married Meagan McGone in Michigan in August 2014. The couple has three children together. [ 16 ] Scaringe stated in 2019 that due to his responsibilities as a start-up founder, he spends approximately 5% of his time with his wife and children. [ 17 ] Scaringe and McGone divorced in 2025. [ 18 ] According to Forbes , Scaringe's net worth was estimated at $3.4 billion as of April 2021, but he lost his billionaire status in March 2022 due to Rivian's declining share value.
