Do not fill this in! ==== UN Global Technical Regulation No. 13 (GTR 13) ====
The foundation of international fuel cell vehicle regulation is UN Global Technical Regulation No. 13 (GTR 13), established on June 27, 2013, under the sponsorship of [[Germany]], [[Japan]], and the [[United States]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Ltd |first=InterRegs |title=InterRegs: Global Technical Regulation No. 13 {{!}} ECE - United Nations |url=https://www.interregs.com/catalogue/details/ECE-GTR13/global-technical-regulation-no-13/hydrogen-and-fuel-cell-vehicles |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240304192951/https://www.interregs.com/catalogue/details/ECE-GTR13/global-technical-regulation-no-13/hydrogen-and-fuel-cell-vehicles |archive-date=2024-03-04 |access-date=2025-07-19 |website=www.interregs.com |language=en}}</ref> GTR 13 applies to all hydrogen-fuelled vehicles of Categories 1-1 and 1-2, with a gross vehicle mass (GVM) of 4,536kg or less. The regulation was developed by the United Nations World Forum for Harmonization of Vehicle Regulations (UN/WP29) with the main purpose of ensuring that hydrogen fuel cell vehicles can achieve the same level of safety as traditional fuel vehicles.<ref>{{Cite journal |last1=Zhu |first1=Shengqing |last2=Ding |first2=Chenglin |last3=Hu |first3=Xing |last4=Tian |first4=Yupeng |date=2024-07-22 |title=Analysis of safety technical standards for hydrogen storage in fuel cell vehicles |journal=Frontiers in Energy Research |language=English |volume=12 |article-number=1376739 |doi=10.3389/fenrg.2024.1376739 |bibcode=2024FrER...1276739Z |doi-access=free |issn=2296-598X}}</ref>

GTR 13 consists of three main sections: high voltage system, hydrogen storage system and hydrogen fuel system at vehicle level. The regulation provides provisions for both in-use and post-crash scenarios.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Ltd |first=InterRegs |title=InterRegs: Global Technical Regulation No. 13 {{!}} ECE - United Nations |url=https://www.interregs.com/catalogue/details/ECE-GTR13/global-technical-regulation-no-13/hydrogen-and-fuel-cell-vehicles |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240304192951/https://www.interregs.com/catalogue/details/ECE-GTR13/global-technical-regulation-no-13/hydrogen-and-fuel-cell-vehicles |archive-date=2024-03-04 |access-date=2025-07-19 |website=www.interregs.com |language=en}}</ref> Phase 2 was adopted at the 190th Session of WP.29 on June 21, 2023, broadening the scope to include heavy-duty vehicles.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-04-17 |title=Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards; Fuel System Integrity of Hydrogen Vehicles; Compressed Hydrogen Storage System Integrity; Incorporation by Reference |url=https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2024/04/17/2024-07116/federal-motor-vehicle-safety-standards-fuel-system-integrity-of-hydrogen-vehicles-compressed |access-date=2025-07-19 |website=Federal Register |language=en}}</ref> Edit summary (Briefly describe your changes) By publishing changes, you agree to the Terms of Use , and you irrevocably agree to release your contribution under the CC BY-SA 4.0 License and the GFDL .
