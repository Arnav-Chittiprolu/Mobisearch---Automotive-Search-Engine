From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia Compact crossover SUV Motor vehicle Hyundai Mufasa Overview Manufacturer Hyundai Model code NU2 Production 2023–present Assembly China: Beijing ( Beijing Hyundai ) Taiwan: Hukou ( Sanyang Motor ) Body and chassis Class Compact crossover SUV Body style 5-door SUV Layout Front-engine, front-wheel-drive Platform Hyundai-Kia N3 Related Hyundai Tucson (NX4) Powertrain Engine Petrol : 2.0 L Smartstream G2.0 MPi I4 Transmission 6-speed automatic Dimensions Wheelbase 2,680 mm (105.5 in) [ 1 ] Length 4,475 mm (176.2 in) [ 1 ] Width 1,850 mm (72.8 in) [ 1 ] Height 1,665–1,685 mm (65.6–66.3 in) [ 1 ] Curb weight 1,440–1,464 kg (3,175–3,228 lb) [ 1 ] Chronology Predecessor Hyundai ix35 (NU) The Hyundai Mufasa ( Chinese : 现代沐飒 ; pinyin : Xiàndài Mùsà ) is a compact crossover SUV produced by the South Korean manufacturer Hyundai through its Beijing Hyundai joint venture in China since 2023. Overview [ edit ] The Mufasa was previewed as a concept car called the Mufasa Adventure on 24 March 2023. [ 2 ] [ 3 ] The concept model is equipped with a range of off-road accessories and rugged design elements. [ 4 ] [ 5 ] [ 6 ] The production model debuted at the 2023 Shanghai Auto Show in April 2023 before going on sale on the Chinese market in June 2023. [ 7 ] [ 8 ] [ 9 ] The Mufasa features a 2.0 L (1,999 cc) engine that produces a power and torque of 118 kW (160 PS) at 6,500 rpm and 193 N⋅m (142 lb⋅ft) at 4,500 rpm, respectively. [ 10 ] Rear view Interior Markets [ edit ] Taiwan [ edit ] The Mufasa was introduced by Hyundai's distributor in Taiwan, Sanyang Motor, on 6 May 2025. It went on sale on 14 May 2025, and it is assembled locally. Three trim levels are offered, which are GLA, GLB, and GLC.
